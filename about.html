About My Project

Hey there! I’m the creator of the International Relations Timeline, a project I kicked off because I’ve always been fascinated by how the world’s history is shaped by diplomacy, conflicts, treaties, and everything in between. I wanted to build something that brings all these events together in one place, making it easy for anyone to dive into the past and see how it connects to today.

Why I Started This

I’ve always had an interest in history, and one day, I thought, “Why not create a timeline that maps out these events, from the 1400s to now, in a way that’s visual and interactive?” So, I started this project to scratch that itch, hoping to share my passion with others who are curious about global relations.

Where the Data Comes From

I pull data from sources like the United Nations and government sites, but what makes this project special is the community. People like you can add events, and I make sure everything is backed by solid sources and info.

How You Can Help

This timeline is a living project, and I’d love for you to be part of it! If you know of an event that belongs here, head over to the Contribute page. Just fill out the form with the details, and I’ll review it to make sure it fits. It’s a team effort to keep this thing growing.
